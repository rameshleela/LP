# Escrow

## Amount Due:
1.	Always try to pick the total unpaid amount because we don't pay by installments. 
2.	Pick Total Amount Due, Balance Due, and Total Outstanding Balance if they are available. Only take Total Premium when no Due amount is specified. 
3.	NEVER take minimum/monthly/quarterly amounts.
4.	For bills that list more than one premium option like Flood Bills, always take the current option or option A.
5.	If there are several options such as the Nationwide Flood Bill take the current coverage amount that matches with the corresponding limit/premium schedule and take that premium as due amount.
6.	For ELIOS forms, never take the Increased Premium, use the smaller amount. 


## Pay of Chk date/Due Date:
1.	Bill / Billresearch/ Billreferenc
1.	Due Date (Note: If document says Due upon Receipt, use Batch Date as the Due Date). 
 
2.	Effective/Renewal Date for the billing term, or Expiration Date for the expiring term. 
3.	If there are only installment due dates on the document, use current date as due date. 
4.	If there's no Due Date, Cancel Date, Effective/Renewal Date indicated on the document, use current date. 

## Expiration Date:
1.	Take the term expiration date of the expire date. 
2.	When document has effective but expire date is not given calculate 12 months from the effective date take it.
